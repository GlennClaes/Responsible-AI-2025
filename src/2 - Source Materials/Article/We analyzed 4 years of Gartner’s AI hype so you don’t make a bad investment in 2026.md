# We analyzed 4 years of Gartner’s AI hype so you don’t make a bad investment in 2026

**Author(s):** Ewelina Lech
**Publication:** Pragmatic Coders Blog
**Date Published:**  August 12, 2025
**URL:**  https://www.pragmaticcoders.com/blog/gartner-ai-hype-cycle

## Summary

The article analyzes four years of Gartner’s _Hype Cycle for Artificial Intelligence_ (2022–2025) to provide insight into the maturity, risks, and realistic investment potential of key AI technologies. It stresses that **real value comes from foundational capabilities**—data readiness, governance, knowledge representation, and engineering discipline—rather than from the latest hype trends.

## Key Points

- The five Gartner phases—**Innovation Trigger**, **Peak of Inflated Expectations**, **Trough of Disillusionment**, **Slope of Enlightenment**, and **Plateau of Productivity**—remain a helpful lens to evaluate AI maturity.
    

### Year-by-Year Overview

- **2022:**  
    Foundation models rise toward their hype peak; focus on operationalizing AI through _Composite AI_ and _Decision Intelligence_.
    
- **2023:**  
    _Generative AI (GenAI)_ reaches the **Peak of Inflated Expectations**. Tools like ChatGPT dominate attention, but enterprise use cases are immature.
    
- **2024:**  
    The “AI hangover” year — scaling and ROI become priorities. GenAI slides into the **Trough of Disillusionment**; _AI Engineering_ and _Knowledge Graphs_ gain traction.
    
- **2025:**
    
    - **Generative AI** → firmly in the **Trough of Disillusionment** (average cost ~$1.9M/project; <30% CEO satisfaction).
    
    - **AI-ready data** and **AI Agents** → at the **Peak of Inflated Expectations**.
    
    - **Knowledge Graphs** → on the **Slope of Enlightenment**, showing growing business value.
    
    - **AI Engineering** → at its own hype peak, but scaling challenges loom.
    
    - **Responsible AI** → peaked; real-world implementation remains complex.
    

---

## Practical Advice

- Don’t chase hype (AI Agents, “AI-ready” data platforms) without a clear pilot and measurable value.

- Lightweight **Knowledge Graphs** act as “cheap insurance” for reliability and governance.

- **AI Engineering (MLOps, DataOps, DevOps)** is non-optional for moving prototypes into production.

- **Responsible AI** must be built in early—bias testing, audit trails, and explainability as part of CI/CD pipelines.

## Responsible AI: 2025–2029 Outlook

|**Technology**|**2025 Position**|**2026**|**2027**|**2028**|**2029**|
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
|**Responsible AI**|Peak of Inflated Expectations|Trough of Disillusionment|Slope of Enlightenment (early)|Slope (mid) – audits standardized|Plateau of Productivity|

### Trend Summary

- **2025:** High expectations—Responsible AI treated as a compliance checkbox.

- **2026:** Disillusionment—bias fixes fail in production, model cards ignored, and regulations (EU AI Act) begin issuing fines.

- **2027–2028:** Slope of Enlightenment—AI Governance Platforms emerge with plug-and-play dashboards, automated bias reporting, and standardized Responsible AI squads.

- **2029:** Plateau—Responsible AI becomes standard practice. Every model must pass fairness, privacy, and explainability checks before release.


---

## Strategic Recommendations

1. **Start building Responsible AI capability now.**  
    Treat fairness, explainability, and governance as integral parts of product quality, not post-hoc compliance.
    
2. **Embed AI TRiSM (Trust, Risk, and Security Management)** directly in your product.  
    Include red-team prompts, audit logs, and bias tests in v1 to reduce regulatory friction.
    
3. **Responsible AI = Faster go-live.**  
    Products with built-in guardrails get **regulatory sign-off faster**, reduce legal risk, and accelerate market entry.
