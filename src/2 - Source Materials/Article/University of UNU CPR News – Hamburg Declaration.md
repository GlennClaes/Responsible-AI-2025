# Hamburg Declaration on Responsible AI for the SDGs

**Author(s):** United Nations University Centre for Policy Research (UNU-CPR)
**Publication:** United Nations University
**Date Published:** June 14, 2025
**URL:**  https://unu.edu/cpr/news/hamburg-declaration-responsible-ai-sdgs

## Summary

The Hamburg Declaration on Responsible Artificial Intelligence for the SDGs is a global commitment to promoting the responsible, inclusive, and sustainable use of AI in advancing the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). Endorsed by the United Nations University (UNU) and co-developed by researchers at UNU-CPR, the Declaration emphasizes a human-centered, rights-based approach to AI development and governance.

## Key Points

- **People:** Promote and protect human rights, gender equality, digital inclusion, and safety, especially for marginalized groups.
    
- **Planet:** Ensure sustainable AI stewardship through green technologies and align with climate action and environmental protection goals.
    
- **Prosperity:** Foster inclusive economic growth by supporting local innovation, skills development, data ecosystems, and equitable access to AI infrastructure.
    
- **Peace:** Safeguard information integrity to build public trust and prevent the misuse of AI for disinformation, social division, or online harm.
    
- **Partnerships:** Encourage multistakeholder collaboration, open data, interdisciplinary learning and research, and AI as a global public good to address shared challenges.
    

The Declaration calls for collective action to seize the opportunities AI offers while minimizing its risks, ensuring that AI contributes positively to the SDGs.
