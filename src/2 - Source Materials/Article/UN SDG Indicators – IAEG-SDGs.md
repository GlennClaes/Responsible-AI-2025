## Summary

The document provides the official global indicators used to monitor progress toward the 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). It lists indicators, their definitions, and methodological guidance to ensure consistent measurement across countries. The indicators are categorized by SDG and include metadata, reporting status, and responsible custodian agencies.


## Key Points

- The SDG indicators are maintained by the IAEG-SDGs and updated periodically to reflect data availability and methodological improvements.
    
- Indicators cover a wide range of areas including poverty, health, education, gender equality, clean water, climate action, and partnerships.
    
- Some indicators are global while others are tiered as Tier I, II, or III based on methodological maturity and data availability.
    
- The document is a reference for national statistical offices, UN agencies, and researchers monitoring SDG progress.
